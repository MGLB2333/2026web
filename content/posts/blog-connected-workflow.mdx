Ask a planner to walk you through how a campaign actually gets built, and you will rarely hear about one tool. You will hear about a chain of them. Audience research in one place, availability checked in another, the plan itself assembled in a spreadsheet, approvals chased over email, and a final report pieced together by hand once the campaign is live.

Each tool does its job. The cost is not in any single step. It is in moving between them.

## The hidden tax on every plan

Every time information crosses from one tool to the next, someone has to carry it. A number is copied, a file is renamed, a version is emailed around. None of it is difficult. All of it is time, and most of it is invisible until you add it up.

<Pull>The work that slows teams down is rarely the planning itself. It is the stitching between tools.</Pull>

That stitching is also where mistakes hide. A figure that was right on Monday is stale by Wednesday. Two people are working from two versions of the same plan without realising. By the time anyone notices, the campaign has already moved on.

## One workflow, end to end

The alternative is not a better spreadsheet or a tidier folder. It is removing the gaps altogether. When audience building, forecasting, trading and analytics share the same workflow, information stops being carried by hand and starts flowing on its own.

- Build an audience and carry it straight into the plan, with no re-keying.
- Forecast reach against live availability, not a snapshot from last week.
- Move from an approved plan to activation without rebuilding it.
- See results against the original plan automatically, as the campaign runs.

## What teams get back

The headline benefit is time. When the busywork disappears, planners get hours back every week, time that goes into strategy instead of reconciliation. But the quieter benefit matters just as much: confidence. One connected workflow means one source of truth, and one source of truth means everyone is finally looking at the same campaign.

That is what LightBoxTV is built to do. Not to add another step, but to connect the ones that already exist into a workflow that holds together on its own.
